Like our squirrel friends - we've cached some facts about our furry friends.

City Slickers & Backcountry Experts

These little guys are often regarded by pedestrians and suburban homeowners as a pest, but we definitely do not see it that way. These critters are some of the most resourcesful, intelligent, and adaptable animals on the planet.

This little guys are quite resilient in all types of enviornments. Being a syanthropic species means over time they have developed to benefit from human habitats and enviornments. But just like us city-dwellers who thrive in an urban enviornment, squirrels are masters of hitting the trails, grasslands and everything in between.

World Wanderers

Squirrels are part of the Sciuridae family - consisting of five different sub-families covering 285 species. These bad boys even have ancestors dating back to the Chadronian period, 40-35 million years ago.

Originally hailing from America, Eurasia, and Africa - Squirrel species can now be found on all continents except Antartica. There is debate over whether Australia is considered to have squirrels. Indrocution of American Grey and the Northern Palm squirrel have occurred but are now considered extinct in Australia with the exception of in zoos.

Crunchy Boys

Most squirrells arre considered scatter-hoarders. The process of burying most of their food in caches is known as "chucking". Research has shown that squirrels are capable of retirveing up to 95% of their chucked food caches. Many consider their style as orginazational chucking - meaning they cache similar food types in one area away from other caches containing different foods.

Squirrels eat a variety of foods varying with each season. Nuts, seeds, conifer cones, fungi, greens, and fruits. Most stick to primarily an herbivore diet, but some have been known to eat smaller animals, snakes, eggs and insects.

Random Facts

Their teeth never stop growing and can average 6 inches of groth a year!

The word 'squirrel' comes from the Greek words "skia" for shadow, and the word "oura" for tail. Together they form the basis of the the naming of their genus Sciurus.

Most squirrels are solitary, but some adults will nest with eachother during the cold winter months. A group of squirrels is called a "scurry" or "dray".

Squirrels will often pretend to bury food, as other squirrels have the habit of spying on others and attempting to steal a easy meal from someone else.
